On Homeschooling

Given the school-age children population of 50 million in the US, how many kids would you guess are homeschooled in the US? I would certainly guess a number below a million. 3-4 million kids are apparently homeschooled in the US. This is probably due to it is being legal and the industry establishing itself around it.

My guess is many people are skeptical of homeschooling. I was too. Yet after becoming a dad, I started asking myself if homeschooling was actually superior.

We do not actively teach our 5 year old anything academic. Due to natural curiosity she learned the Cyrillic letters and can read basic words. Obviously there are much more capable 5 year olds out there, my point is if a kid can learn basic stuff without active teaching - imagine things she can learn in 1-2 hour/day pace with a teacher. I believe 2 hours a day is enough to master the current school curriculum. Evidence to that is - Alpha School in the US, which has 2 hour academic sessions a day, and the rest of the day spent doing fun stuff. Alpha School students score very high in state exams compared to peers.

Benefits of homeschooling is twofold in my mind: 1) Pacing of academic materials tailored to your kids' abilities, 2) frees up time for your kid to do other stuff (playing, extracurricular activities etc).

To be clear, we are not planning to homeschool. Homeschooling is a very serious undertaking that requires strong commitment from parents. I am not sure if we are ready for such a commitment. Frankly, I am not sure if it's legal in Kazakhstan. Just wanted to highlight that we don't discuss homeschooling enough in the society.
